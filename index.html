Our Story

I immigrated to the Philippines to be closer to my wife, and once I was here, I was fascinated with the wide world of kakanin. After months of experimentation, I'm looking forward to offer comforting native snacks with classic and novel flavors to the placid city of Marbel.

What piqued my interest most of all was the traditional rice cake: putong bigas. In its most authentic form, puto is made from galapong: rice batter that has been left to naturally ferment, developing a unique aroma and flavor over time. I tweaked each variable to find exactly which rice to use, how much liquid it needs, how long to ferment, and more, in order to finally find the recipe for what I think may be puto perfection.
